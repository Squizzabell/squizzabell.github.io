Iguanas are herbivorous reptiles native to Central and South America, as well as the Caribbean. They are cold-blooded and regulate their body temperature by basking in the sun or seeking shade. Iguanas have a unique "third eye" on top of their heads, which detects light changes and helps them avoid predators. Many species have spiny crests along their backs for communication and defense. They can live up to 20-30 years in captivity and have the ability to drop their tails to escape danger, with the tail eventually regrowing.
